Homelessness

Lack of treatment for the most seriously mentally ill causes the kind of delusions and bizarre behavior that makes living alone or at home with families untenable. As a result, many become people with untreated serious mental illness become homeless and communities are forced to bear the cost of that.

Substance use and addiction

People with schizophrenia may be at higher risk of drug abuse for a variety of reasons. One reason is that some people with schizophrenia may use drugs as a way to self-medicate and try to alleviate their symptoms, such as hallucinations, delusions, or disorganized thinking. Substance abuse can also be a coping mechanism for people with schizophrenia who are struggling with the challenges of living with the condition, such as social isolation or difficulty functioning in daily life. Additionally, people with schizophrenia may be more prone to risky behavior and impulsive decision-making, which can increase their risk of drug abuse.

Dangerous or self-harming behaviors

individuals with schizophrenia may be more at risk of self-harm due to the symptoms of their condition, such as hallucinations, delusions, or extreme changes in mood. For example, a person with schizophrenia may experience hallucinations or delusions that lead them to believe that self-harm is necessary or desirable. Additionally, people with schizophrenia may experience extreme changes in mood or intense feelings of distress, which may lead them to engage in self-harm as a way of coping with these emotions.

Suicide attempts or thoughts of suicide

Suicide is a serious and potentially life-threatening complication of schizophrenia and other mental illnesses. People with schizophrenia may be at increased risk for suicide due to the extreme distress and suffering caused by the symptoms of the disorder, as well as the social isolation and stigma that may result from having the disorder.
